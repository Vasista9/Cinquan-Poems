Meaning:

A cinquain poem (pronounced sin-kquane) is a five-line unrhymed poem following a specific structure, often focusing on a subject, with common forms using syllable counts (like 2-4-6-8-2 for Adelaide Crapsey's style) or parts of speech (line1 - noun, line2 - two adjectives, line3 - three -ing verbs, line4 - a four word feeling phrase, line5 - synonym of the first line.)
